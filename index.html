About Jongmyo Shrine
Gyeongbokgung Palace was the largest palace built during the Joseon Dynasty. Completed in 1395, the third year of the founding of the Joseon Dynasty, the castle has 390 rooms and is located at the center of Hanyang. Jeong Do-jeon, the country's founder, was commanded by King Taejo to name the first palace. After much deliberation, Gyeongbokgung Palace is the largest of Palace in the Joseon Dynasty. Gwanghwamun is the main Gyeongbokgung's entrance on the south way. It means 'the king's great virtue illuminates the whole country. In 1395, Gyeongbokgung was built. There are three Hongyemun (Achimun Gate) in the stone part of Gwanghwamun. The middle gate was the gate for the king, and the other left and suitable gates were for the servants. Also, there are many traditional places and activities. Jongmyo is a Confucian shrine where the gods of the successive kings and queens of the Joseon dynasty are enshrined, and rites are held. It is located at 1 Hunjeong-dong, Jongno-gu, Seoul, and has been designated as a historic site. In terms of location, it is adjacent to Changdeokgung and Changgyeonggung. Also, if you go there, you can try to wear traditional Korean cloths and experience traditional activities
